# Does Silver Tarnish?

Yes, silver can tarnish over time. Tarnish is common and usually manageable with simple care habits.

## Quick Answer

- Silver tarnish is normal.
- Tarnish speed depends on moisture, air exposure, and usage.
- Regular cleaning and storage reduce visible darkening.

## 1. Why Tarnish Happens

Tarnish usually forms when silver reacts with sulfur-containing compounds and environmental exposure.

It is a surface reaction, not a sign that the metal is fake.

---

## 2. What Speeds Up Tarnish

| Trigger | Impact |
|---|---|
| Humidity | Faster surface darkening |
| Perfume/chemicals | Increased reaction risk |
| Sweat and skin oils | Can accelerate discoloration |
| Improper storage | More oxidation exposure |

---

## 3. How to Slow Tarnish

- Store dry in anti-tarnish pouches
- Wipe after each wear
- Avoid direct chemical contact
- Clean on a regular schedule

## 4. Can Tarnished Silver Be Restored?

Most tarnish can be cleaned using suitable silver-care methods. Severe tarnish may need professional service.

## Advanced Care Insights

Long-term results with jewellery usually come from small, consistent habits rather than occasional deep cleaning.

Prioritize this order:

1. Preventive care (remove during high-risk exposure)
2. Light routine cleaning (quick wipe after use)
3. Correct storage (dry, separated, low-humidity)
4. Scheduled deeper maintenance as needed

Most avoidable damage is caused by repeated exposure to chemicals, moisture retention, and rough storage. A small weekly routine generally preserves appearance better than infrequent heavy treatment.

## Expert Maintenance Checklist

- Keep jewellery dry after contact with water or sweat
- Avoid direct contact with perfumes and harsh cleaners
- Clean gently on a predictable schedule
- Store in a dry, separate, anti-tarnish setup
- Inspect clasps/settings periodically
