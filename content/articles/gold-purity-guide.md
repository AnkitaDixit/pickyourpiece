## 1. What Gold Purity Means

Purity is the share of pure gold in a jewellery alloy.

Higher karat means more pure gold, but not always better daily-use performance.

---

## 2. Purity Table You Can Use While Shopping

| Karat | Pure Gold % | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| 9KT | 37.5% | Budget and practical wear |
| 14KT | 58.5% | Daily wear and engagement balance |
| 18KT | 75% | Premium look and richer tone |
| 22KT | 91.6% | Traditional jewellery, less common for stone-heavy daily rings |
| 24KT | 99.9% | Investment/bullion, rarely used for complex ring settings |

---

## 3. Purity vs Durability Reality

As purity rises, metal can become softer for high-friction daily use.

That is why 14KT is frequently recommended for rings worn every day.

## 4. Purity vs Color

Higher karat yellow gold often appears richer and deeper.

Lower karat options may look lighter but can perform better for active lifestyles.

## 5. How to Choose Purity by Scenario

- Daily ring: 14KT usually best
- Budget-focused gift: 9KT or 14KT
- Luxury rich-tone preference: 18KT
- Engagement ring with active use: 14KT shortlist first

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming higher purity is always best
- Ignoring your real wear pattern
- Skipping hallmark checks
- Not comparing same design across karats

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is 22KT better than 18KT for rings?

Not always. For many ring designs, lower karat can be more practical for daily wear.

---

### Which purity is best for engagement rings?

For most buyers, 14KT offers the best balance.

---

### Does higher purity mean better investment?

For jewellery, craftsmanship and making charges matter too. Purity is only one part.

---

### How do I verify purity?

Check hallmark details and ensure invoice purity matches the product claim.

## Deeper Expert Notes

Before finalizing any jewellery decision, evaluate ownership beyond day-one appearance. Long-term satisfaction usually depends on three factors: how well the item suits your routine, how predictable maintenance will be, and how confidently the seller supports post-purchase issues.

Ask these practical questions before payment:

- What type of wear pattern is this product designed for?
- Which signs of normal wear should I expect in 6 to 12 months?
- What cleaning method is safe for this specific construction?
- What repairs or maintenance are covered, and for how long?
- Is resize, exchange, or replacement straightforward if needed?

These questions reduce avoidable regret and help you compare products on real ownership quality, not only marketing copy.

---

## Questions to Ask the Seller

Use this quick script while shortlisting:

1. Can you confirm the exact material/purity specification on invoice?
2. What is the recommended care frequency for this item?
3. Which usage conditions should I avoid?
4. What is the return or exchange window?
5. Is there any paid or free service support after purchase?

If answers are unclear, treat that as a warning sign and continue comparing alternatives.
