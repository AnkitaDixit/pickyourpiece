## 1. How Much Should You Spend on an Engagement Ring?

Ignore the old "three months salary" rule. A better approach is to set a number that does not strain your cash flow or debt plan.

Use this baseline:

- Safe range: one-time spend you can cover without EMI stress
- Stretch range: only if savings remain healthy after purchase
- Do-not-cross limit: your maximum comfort boundary

Budget confidence is more important than headline carat.

## 3. Split Your Budget Intentionally

A practical allocation model:

- 60% to 75%: center stone
- 20% to 35%: setting and metal
- 5% to 10%: sizing, certification, maintenance buffer

If sparkle is your priority, put more into cut quality. If design impact is your priority, reserve more for setting and band craftsmanship.

---

## 4. Where to Spend for Maximum Visible Impact

If budget is tight, prioritize what the eye notices first:

1. Cut quality (sparkle)
2. Shape and spread (face-up size look)
3. Setting style coherence
4. Metal and finish details

## 5. Smart Ways to Save Without Looking Cheap

- Choose near-threshold weights (for example 0.90 ct vs 1.00 ct)
- Pick near-colorless range for value balance
- Prefer eye-clean clarity over premium grades invisible to naked eye
- Compare lab-grown vs natural where appropriate
- Consider halo/cluster styles for bigger visual presence

## 7. Do Not Forget Lifecycle Costs

Add a post-purchase buffer to avoid regret:

- Ring resizing
- Annual cleaning/polish
- Rhodium replating (for white gold)
- Insurance (optional but useful for higher budgets)

A ring that is affordable to maintain is better than one that is expensive to own.

---

## 8. Compare Before You Buy (This Saves the Most)

Always compare at least 3 similar rings with the same budget and design intent.

Checklist for comparisons:

- Price vs total specs
- Certification clarity
- Metal purity and finish
- Return and exchange policy
- Hidden add-on costs

## 9. Common Budget Mistakes to Avoid

- Setting budget based on social pressure
- Spending heavily on carat while ignoring cut
- Not reserving maintenance buffer
- Choosing style first without price benchmarking
- Finalizing the first ring without cross-brand comparison

---

## 10. 60-Second Budget Decision Framework

Use this sequence for fast clarity:

1. Set your comfort cap.
2. Pick your top priority: sparkle, size look, or design statement.
3. Shortlist two styles in the same budget.
4. Compare 3 to 5 options with similar specs.
5. Select the one with strongest visual impact per rupee.
