# Which Diamond Looks Biggest? What Actually Drives Visual Size

The biggest-looking diamond is not always the one with highest carat.

This guide explains how shape, spread, and setting combine to create face-up size illusion.

## 1. Carat vs Face-Up Spread

Carat is weight, not visual width.

Face-up dimensions and cut quality drive perceived size more strongly.

This is why two diamonds with identical carat can look noticeably different when worn.

## 2. Shape Size Illusion Table

| Shape | Perceived Size | Why |

| Oval | High | Elongated face-up profile |

| Round | Medium | Classic but compact geometry |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which shape looks biggest per carat?

Marquise and oval are common winners.

### Do halos make diamonds look bigger?

Yes, often significantly.

### Is round the smallest-looking shape?

Not always, but it can look more compact than elongated shapes.

### What should I prioritize for bigger look?

Shape spread, cut, and setting proportion.

### Does halo always make a diamond look bigger?

Usually yes, but proportion matters. A poorly sized halo can look crowded.

### Is a bigger-looking diamond always a better choice?

Not necessarily. Balance visual size with sparkle quality and daily comfort.
